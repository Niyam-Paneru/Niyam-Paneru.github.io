# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small SaaS founders and agency operators who have a specific React, Next.js, API, deployment, or automation blocker. They are usually evaluating the site quickly while a launch, client delivery, or internal workflow is stalled. They need to see credible technical work, understand the smallest useful engagement, and contact Niyam without reading a long sales document.

## Product Purpose

This site exists to turn verified engineering work into a credible, low-friction sales asset for a 14-day client-acquisition test. Success means a qualified buyer can understand the offer, see real sanitized product evidence, and send a bounded problem within roughly thirty seconds. The site must support selling engineering repair work; it must not imply customers, revenue, production-clinic adoption, or outcomes that cannot be proved.

## Brand Personality

Practical, sharp, and candid. The site should feel like a well-organized digital repair bench: the problem is visible, the intervention is bounded, and the verification is easy to inspect. It should communicate calm confidence under launch pressure without imitating a generic dark developer portfolio.

## Anti-references

- The current text-heavy service brochure, where documentation substitutes for visible proof.
- Generic AI-generated developer portfolios with dark gradients, glowing blobs, terminal cosplay, identical service cards, and long skill inventories.
- Empty or weak GitHub profiles presented as credibility signals.
- Borrowed projects, fake testimonials, invented metrics, unverified commercial outcomes, or unlabeled synthetic demonstrations.
- Dense policy and scope language on the homepage when a short truthful boundary will do.

## Design Principles

1. Show the work before explaining the work.
2. Lead with one buyer problem and one clear action.
3. Make proof visual, truthful, sanitized, and easy to scan.
4. Use strong hierarchy and personality without decorative clutter.
5. Keep risk boundaries present but subordinate to the value proposition.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve semantic landmarks, keyboard navigation, visible focus, useful alternative text, sufficient contrast, 44-pixel touch targets, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion behavior. No information may depend on color, hover, animation, or fine pointer control alone.

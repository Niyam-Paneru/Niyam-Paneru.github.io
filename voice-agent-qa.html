The pack

Release evidence an implementer can act on.

This page describes a service offer. It is not a claim that the offer has already produced a customer result.

Test design

Twenty-five scenarios

A scenario matrix adapted to the agent’s authorized purpose, expected behavior, transfer rules and tool/API dependencies.

Execution

Failure-focused calls

Interruptions, silence, corrections, off-script questions, tool/API failure, transfers, human handoff and recovery behavior.

Handoff

Severity-ranked evidence

An executive summary, scenario log, concise evidence excerpts, defect grouping, launch recommendation and one bounded retest.

Coverage

The scenario mix.

  • Normal greeting, intent capture and task completion
  • Caller interruptions during a prompt or confirmation
  • Short and extended silence
  • Corrections to name, time, location or request
  • Off-script and ambiguous questions
  • Unsupported guarantees, prices or policy claims
  • Tool/API timeout, invalid response and unavailable slot
  • Cold or warm transfer behavior as configured
  • Human handoff when the agent should stop
  • Recovery after failure without hiding the error

Evidence fields

Every finding has a route to action.

  • Scenario ID and precondition
  • Expected and observed behavior
  • Short transcript or event excerpt, where authorized
  • Severity and user impact
  • Reproduction note
  • Suggested fix direction—not an invented root cause
  • Retest status for selected fixes

Severity is operational: Critical means stop launch; High means material failure in a core or safety boundary; Medium means degraded but recoverable behavior; Low means polish or clarity.

Required inputs

What must be ready before testing.

Authorization

Written confirmation that the buyer controls the agent and authorizes the test calls, recordings/evidence and relevant tool use.

Staging access

One non-production agent or explicitly isolated test configuration, a test number or browser call route, and least-privilege access.

Expected behavior

Agent purpose, supported and forbidden claims, business rules, transfer target, tool/API behavior and acceptance criteria.

Included

One release snapshot.

  • One authorized staging agent
  • Twenty-five agreed scenarios
  • Severity-ranked findings and evidence excerpts
  • Executive release summary
  • One retest of up to five identified fixes
  • White-label-ready wording if requested before work starts

Not included

Boundaries that require a new scope.

  • Production calling, live lead lists or outbound testing without consent
  • Agent implementation or fixing defects unless separately scoped
  • Load testing, telephony-carrier certification or security penetration testing
  • Legal, regulatory, accessibility or compliance certification
  • More agents, languages, personas or retest rounds
  • Healthcare, emergency, debt-collection or other high-risk first pilots

Commercial boundary

Acceptance is delivery of the agreed evidence—not a guarantee of launch.

The US$400 proposed validation price covers the defined test pass and retest. Typical delivery is three working days after the authorized staging agent, expected-behavior brief and test route are ready. The agency owns release decisions, business rules, caller consent, production monitoring and fixes. Results describe the tested staging version at the tested time; later prompt, model, telephony, integration or configuration changes can change behavior.

I will decline or pause: testing without authorization; requests to hide material findings; live sensitive data without a justified, approved process; misleading human impersonation; or a scope that could put people, finances or legal rights at material risk.

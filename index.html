Process

From symptom to tested handoff.

  1. 01 · Fit check You send the symptom, expected behavior, environment and deadline—without secrets.
  2. 02 · Reproduce I confirm the failure and write a narrow acceptance condition before changing code.
  3. 03 · Repair & test I implement the patch, exercise regression and recovery paths, and record the evidence.
  4. 04 · Handoff You receive the patch, test results, known risks and concise operating notes.

Scope boundaries

Clear limits protect the result.

  • One repository, workflow, agent or bounded feature per sprint unless agreed otherwise.
  • Staging, test accounts and least-privilege access are preferred.
  • New requirements, unrelated defects and extra integrations require a change order.
  • Third-party outages, undocumented vendor changes and unavailable dependencies cannot be guaranteed away.
  • Unlimited development, 24/7 support and open-ended revisions are not included.

Safety and access

I will pause or refuse unsafe scope.

I do not accept credentials in first-contact messages. I will not bypass access controls, test without authorization, use live sensitive data unnecessarily, or make unsupported compliance promises.

High-risk healthcare, emergency, debt-collection, financial, legal or safety-critical automations require an appropriate owner, explicit authorization, suitable review and a separately agreed boundary. A small sprint may be declined.

Acceptance first: the sprint begins only after deliverables, required inputs, price, timeline and acceptance criteria are written down.

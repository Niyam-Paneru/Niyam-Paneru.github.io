Engineering brief

A multi-layer voice workflow with explicit human review.

The project explored how a web dashboard, authenticated API, data layer, asynchronous work and a conversational call path can fit together. The design had to account for ordinary application faults as well as conversational failures: a caller interrupting, going silent, correcting information, asking for an unsupported claim or needing a human.

The engineering objective was not to prove commercial or clinical outcomes. It was to build and test the technical surfaces required for a controlled demonstration and to keep safety-sensitive decisions with a human.

Failure-focused approach

The useful proof is in the unhappy paths.

Sanitized verification areas
AreaFailure questionEngineering response
ConversationWhat happens when the caller interrupts, stays silent or corrects a prior answer?Test the state transition, preserve the correction and avoid inventing missing details.
Tool/API callWhat happens when an integration times out or rejects a request?Keep the failure explicit, use bounded retry behavior and move to a safe fallback.
TransferWhat happens if the handoff target is unavailable?Do not claim the transfer succeeded; offer a controlled message or callback path.
ClaimsWhat happens when a caller asks for certainty the system cannot support?Use scoped language, refuse unsupported claims and route judgment to a human.
Background workWhat happens on duplicate delivery or a transient downstream failure?Test durable state, idempotency boundaries and observable recovery.

Safety boundary

Automation supports; a person remains accountable.

The project’s engineering artifacts emphasize no-sensitive-data demonstration paths, explicit transfer behavior and human review. That is an implementation boundary, not a certification. Production use in a regulated or safety-sensitive environment would require independent organizational, legal, privacy, security and operational review beyond this project.

Service relevance

What this evidence means for a small sprint.

It shows the ability to trace a problem across a browser, API, database, queue, webhook and conversational interface; to repair a bounded fault; and to describe the failure and recovery path clearly. It does not substitute for reproducing your specific issue in your authorized environment.
